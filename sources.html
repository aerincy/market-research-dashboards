Space Insurance Market Dynamics

The space insurance market represents both a key customer segment and a structural driver for IDI's technology. The global market is valued at $4.43 billion in 2026, with a projected 9.1% CAGR reaching $6.23 billion by 2030, with North America (41%), Europe (26%), and Asia-Pacific (24%) as the primary regional segments. The market experienced significant volatility in 2023, with $995 million in total payouts against approximately $550 million in premiums. Despite these losses, the market is stabilizing with four new London market participants entering in 2025.

International Policy & Regulation

Regulatory frameworks play a critical role in the adoption of safety technologies. The historical precedent for mandated safety recording is found in the aviation sector, where flight data recorders were mandated between 1958 and 1965 following a series of unexplained accidents. In the commercial space sector, the FAA mandates financial responsibility and third-party liability insurance under 14 CFR Part 450. Germany has committed to a €35 billion military space investment (2026–2030), while Singapore has allocated an S$60 million fund to its Space Technology Development Programme (STDP).
